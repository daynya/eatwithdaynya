How to fuel for performance and life

Tips to make your nutrition work for your goals.

What is the best diet?

The best diet is the one you can follow consistently. You can achieve short term success with any number of restrictive eating methods. What we're looking for is what you can do repeatedly, consistently, and sustainably. What is a way of eating that not only supports your goals, but also supports your lifestyle?

There are no shortcuts

So often we buy into the hype that is displayed in media or online and we want to find a quick fix. Unfortunately, there are not any quick fixes that actually fix anything at all. Showing up every day and doing the work can feel boring and hard, but that is the only true shortcut to your goals.

Consistency is King

Any diet can work in the short term, but our bodies do best when we provide them with what they need, consistently. That is not to say you should be striving for perfection all of the time. Quite the opposite - the key to consistency is balance. You need to make sure that you are ENJOYING your life, or you will burn out on your lifestyle - no matter how successful it may feel. Aim for being on track with your goals 80% of the time. Allow yourself the other 20% to find the space to enjoy your life.

It's not just calories in, calories out

Our bodies are not machines. There is a common misconception that we all need to eat less and move more and we'll magically solve all of life's problems. The reality is, it depends on what your goals are. If you are looking to improve stretgth, increase muscle, ramp up metabolism, improve sleep...well you may actually be surprised to learn that you might need more fuel than you're getting.

Supplement Last

There are some great supplements out there that most people can benefit from adding into their diet. But these are to fill the gaps. The majority of our nutrients should be coming from whole foods. Focus your efforts on getting your micro and macronutrients from your diet, and then if there are things that are missing, add in supplements to round things out.
